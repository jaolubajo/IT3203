What Are Cookies?

A cookie is a small piece of data stored on a user's device, designed to remember information across sessions and preferences. Cookies are essential for providing personalized web experiences.

Cookies were first introduced in 1994, in collaboration with the Netscape Navigator web browser. Initially, they were used to track whether a person had visited a website before. Over time, their use has expanded, and they are now integral to many web applications, such as keeping users logged in or remembering their language preferences.
